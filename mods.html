By Charlotte Whyte
IMAGES MODIFICATIONS REPAIRS HISTORY
To Begin
My 1991 Toyota MR2 has had little modifications due to it needing lots of basics fixed, as well as maintenance. I also am not looking to spend thousands on changing the car, such as body-kits and engine modifications
Head Unit
When i bought the car, it had a really old fashioned head unit, that i was unable to connect my phone to. I bought a $600 kenwood double din headunit from supercheap auto and installed it.
Steering Wheel
I really like the design of my steering wheel so I'm not looking to buy an aftermarket removeable one, atleast not for a while. The stock steering wheel had lots of wear and unevenness, so i sanded and filled the wheel to make it smooth, then sewed a leather cover on.
Exhaust
I spent about 4 hours working on restoring my exhaust. I begun by removing it and found that there was a small hole near my muffler, as well as rusty bolts holding it on. I wetsanded from 400 inccrementally up to 2000 grit and then polished with a random orbital machine.
Shifter and Shift Boot
When I first bought my car, the plastic surrounding the shifter was cracked, so I patched and resprayed it (no image). The original shifter and boot was very worn and I wasn't the biggest fan. I bought a heavy weight, "LikeWise" shifter, as well as sewed my own custom shift boot.
Wheels and Coilovers
The wheels that came with the car were 17inch Enkei Sc03, with very uevenly worn re003 tyres, gutter rash and bad fitment. I ordered custom SSR SP1R wheels from AutoTechnik, which took 6 months to arrive from Japan. I had to install BC coilovers to fit the wheels due to their wider sizing. The car's fenders also needed to be rolled so the wheels wouldnt rub as bad.

Thanks for visiting :)
